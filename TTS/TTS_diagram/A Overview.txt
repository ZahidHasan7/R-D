This diagram illustrates the Bangla TTS (Text-to-Speech) High-Level Pipeline, which is divided into three primary functional blocks: Normalization, Contextual Refinement, and Audio Synthesis.

Here is the step-by-step explanation of the workflow:

Phase 1: Hybrid Normalization Engine
This block handles the conversion of non-standard "raw" text (like "৫kg" or "Dr.") into full Bengali words.

Unicode Normalization (NFC): The raw text is first standardized to the NFC (Normalization Form Canonical Composition) to ensure characters like Bengali hoshonto or kar are consistent.
Decision Engine & Classifier: Each token is classified into categories:
UNIT: Routed to the Unit Handler (e.g., 5kg → ৫ কেজি).
ABBREV: Routed to the Abbreviation Handler (e.g., Dr. → ডাক্তার).
MIXED: Routed to a Token Splitter if a word contains both digits and letters (e.g., 10GB), which then feeds back into the decision engine for individual processing.
Rule-Based Expansion: General tokens (Numbers, Dates, Currency, and Time) are expanded using logic-based scripts (e.g., ১০ → দশ).
NER (Named Entity Recognition) Handler: Processes brand names or specific entities that require unique mappings or should be preserved.
Phase 2: Contextual Refinement & G2P
This block focuses on the phonetic representation and handling complex "Banglish" or code-mixed text.

ML Translator (BanglaT5): A decision is made (Use ML?). If the text contains English or complex code-mixed segments, the BanglaT5 machine learning model translates/normalizes it into natural Bengali.
Hybrid G2P Engine: This converts the normalized Bengali text into a Phoneme Stream using a 4-layered fallback strategy:
Layer 1 (Dictionary): Instant lookup for known words.
Layer 2 (Rule-Based Mapping): Applies phonetic rules if the word isn't in the dictionary.
Layer 3 (Neural G2P /byt5/): A neural model predicts pronunciation for rare or unknown words.
Layer 4 (Phoneme Stream): The final output is a sequence of phonetic tokens.
Phase 3: Audio Synthesis
The final conversion from phonemes to a playable audio file.

Prosody Formatter: Adjusts the rhythm and timing (e.g., adding pauses at commas or full stops) to make the speech sound natural.
TTS Engine Selection:
Google Cloud TTS (gTTS): Used as a fast cloud-based fallback.
VITS2 Model: A high-fidelity, local neural model used for premium, natural-sounding Bengali voice generation.
Audio Output: The result is rendered into the final audio file (typically .wav or .mp3).


This diagram detailed the Step 1: Token Classification logic, which is the foundational sorting mechanism in the Hybrid Normalization Engine.

Workflow Breakdown:
Input Token: The process starts with a single segment of text (a word or number) chunked from the raw input.
Regex Pattern matching: Each token is parsed through a decision diamond that uses Regular Expressions to determine its nature:
MIXED (Digit + String): Identifying tokens that combine numbers and characters, such as 10GB or ২০১২-১৩.
ABBREVIATION (JSON Keys match): The token is checked against a JSON dictionary of known shortcuts (e.g., Dr. or etc.).
UNIT (Unit list match): Specific matches for technical units like kg, meter, or ৳.
NORMAL (None of the above): If the token doesn't trigger any special rules, it is categorized as standard text that doesn't require complex expansion.
This classification ensures that every piece of text is routed to the correct specialized handler in the next stage.



This diagram covers Steps 2 and 3 of the pipeline, focusing on how different types of tokens are transformed into fully expanded Bengali text.

Phase 2: Specialist Processing
Once a token is classified, it moves into a dedicated handler for precise translation:

UNIT Handler: Specifically designed for measurements. For example, it converts the shorthand 5kg into the natural Bengali speech form ৫ কেজি.
ABBREVIATION Handler: Expands standard short-forms into their full spoken titles, such as Dr. becoming ডাক্তার.
TokenSplitter (for MIXED tokens): For complex strings like 10GB, the splitter breaks them into 10 and GB. These are then sent back through the Decision Engine (Recursion) to be handled as a number and a unit separately.
Phase 3: Semantic Expansion
After the specialist handlers do their work, the text passes through a sequence of grammar-heavy normalization rules:

Month/Date Rules: Converts numeric dates or calendar-specific strings into natural Bengali phrasing.
Currency Rules: Translates symbols (like ৳) and amounts into spoken words (e.g., ৫০০ টাকা).
Number Rules: The core engine for cardinal/ordinal number conversion (e.g., ৫০ → পঞ্চাশ).
NER Handler: The "Named Entity Recognition" check ensures that specific proper nouns, brand names, or technical terms are mapped to their correct phonetic Bengali equivalents before synthesis.


Explaning Step 4: Hybrid G2P Engine

This diagram illustrates Step 4: The Hybrid G2P (Grapheme-to-Phoneme) Engine, which converts written Bengali text into its phonetic representation through a 4-layered fallback system.

The 4-Layer Fallback Strategy:
Layer 1: Dict Lookup (High Speed):
The engine first checks a pre-compiled pronunciation dictionary. If the word matches, it returns the phonemes instantly. If not (a "Miss"), it proceeds to the next layer.
Layer 2: Rule Mapping (Deterministic Logic):
This layer applies linguistic rules for Bengali pronunciation:
Consonant Map: Maps standard characters to their phonetic equivalents.
Vowel/Matra Map: Processes vowels and their diacritic forms.
Hasanta Suppression: Handles the removal of inherent vowels at the end of syllables or words, a common feature in Bengali.
Layer 4: Neural Model (/byt5/):
If the rules cannot confidently resolve the pronunciation (a "Miss"), the system uses a fine-tuned ByT5 neural model. This model "imagines" the pronunciation character by character, making it ideal for rare names, new loanwords, or creative spellings.
Layer 4: Fallback Original:
As a safety net, if the neural model fails, the system returns the original word to ensure the synthesis pipeline continues without crashing.
This hybrid approach balances the speed of dictionaries, the reliability of rules, and the intelligence of AI.


This diagram illustrates Step 5: Synthesis & Prosody, the final stage where phonetic data becomes human-like sound.

Final Synthesis Flow:
Phoneme Stream: This stage receives the cleaned phonetic tokens from the G2P engine.
Prosody Formatter: This module adds "life" to the speech. It interprets punctuation (like full stops । or commas ,) and linguistic context to insert silence durations, pitch variations, and rhythm markers. This prevents the voice from sounding robotic.
Engine Selection: A choice is made based on performance needs or configuration:
Cloud (GTTS Backend): Standard Google Cloud TTS used for fast generation or as a reliable backup.
Local (VITS2 Backend): The primary high-performance neural engine that runs on the local server, providing more natural, expressive, and high-fidelity Bengali voices.
Waveform Generation: The chosen engine synthesizes the actual audio waves, resulting in a playable .wav or .mp3 file.

This concludes the 5-step breakdown of the Bangla TTS Pipeline. Every stage, from raw text cleaning to neural audio synthesis, works in harmony to produce high-quality Bengali speech.
